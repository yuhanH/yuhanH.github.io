Intro

I completed my PhD at New York University and New York Genome Center advised by Rahul Satija. I am currently the lead developer of Seurat, a widely used toolkit for single-cell genomics data analysis (>1.5M downloads, June 2023). My doctoral research primarily is to to develop computational algorithms to learn the representation of single-cell data. This contained four primary analytical challenges:

  • How can we construct a single-cell reference when measuring multiple modalities from an individual cell? [Weighted Nearest Neighbors]
  • When dealing with data from millions of cells, how do we effectively build the reference? [Atomic Sketch Integration]
  • How can we map new query data onto a curated reference? [ Single-cell Reference Mapping ]
  • How do we align query data with the reference when they capture different measurements? [ Bridge Integration]
